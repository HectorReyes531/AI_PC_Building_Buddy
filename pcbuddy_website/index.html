White Fusion

This was my first ever time building and owning a PC. I have primarily gamed on Xbox since I was 11 but I am about to graduate college now and not only wanted to upgrade from Xbox to play more games and better gaming experience, but also to use for mapping on GIS and to do work/schoolwork on. I have had the monitor for a while now but I will be getting a new one soon with an increased frame rate. Ended up working out better than I anticipated, although I think I will most likely add a few more fans eventually, although it is not a major concern.

ライゼン - (Ry) Zen

The build primarily incorporates local Australian timbers – Jarrah and Tasmanian Oak. These can be found on the external veneer as well as the Shoji (screen), GPU backplate and panels for cable concealment (above motherboard). The main section of Shoji provides an aesthetic eye catcher inside / outside - looks good, distracts the viewer as well as conceals the case suction fans and cabling when viewing the case from the front. It also doubles as a funnel to force cool air towards the CPU cooler and GPU in what is otherwise a poorly rated case for airflow / cooling. The “Yukimi” (snow viewing) Shoji provides a different viewpoint of the Zen garden when viewing from the front of the case while serving its true purpose as a support stand for the heavy GPU. The main Shoji relies on acrylic panels for light diffusion / air-tight whereas the Yukimi Shoji uses traditional Shoji “Washi” paper.

Cyber PC

When a friend of mine asked me to build him a clean PC I jumped at the chance to make something slick and pristine. Working within his budget of $2500.00 dollars we worked on making a clean, capable and powerful gaming PC. Using parts I had used many times before I think this came out crisp and clean. No issues with this build at all. Build went smooth and I love building in Phanteks cases. Only issue I am having is fan speed control but am working through that before making delivery.
